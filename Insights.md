📌 Key Insights — YouTube Trending Analytics (US, Nov 2017 – Jun 2018)

1. 📊 Views Are Highly Skewed — Median Tells a Truer Story
The average trending video gets 1.96M views, but the median is only 518K — just 26% of the mean. A small number of viral outliers inflate the average dramatically. For any benchmarking, median is the right metric, not mean.

2. 🎮 Gaming Punches Far Above Its Weight
Gaming represents only 1.6% of trending content yet achieves the highest median views at 1.3M — roughly 2.5× the overall median. A tiny category delivering outsized results suggests extremely loyal, high-engagement audiences.

3. 🎵 Music Is the Only Dual Champion
Music is the only category that ranks in the top 3 for both raw views AND engagement rate simultaneously. Every other high-view category either lacks engagement depth, or high-engagement categories lack raw reach.

4. 📰 News & Politics — High Frequency, Low Reward
News & Politics trends frequently (high volume of trending appearances) but delivers 3× fewer views than the dataset median. News gets onto the trending list easily but doesn't sustain viewership — people watch one clip and move on.

5. ⏱️ Slow-Burn Content Beats Fast News Spikes
Videos that take longer to reach trending status accumulate more total views (correlation: +0.345). Fast-trending news videos spike and fade. Slower-rising entertainment/gaming content builds over time and ultimately outperforms. Evergreen > ephemeral.

6. 🗂️ Data Quality Was Strong After Deduplication
The raw dataset had 40,949 rows, but after removing duplicates (same video appearing on the trending list on multiple days), only 6,351 unique videos remained. This is critical — naive analysis on raw rows would skew every category metric by counting popular videos multiple times.
